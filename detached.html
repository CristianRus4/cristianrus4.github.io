Detached

Internet is the technology that has most influenced my life. I live for and thanks to Internet. It has allowed me to grow as a person and professionally to unimaginable levels. I love Internet. And at the same time, I hate it with all my strength.

For years I have tried to leave Internet. Sometimes I have taken breaks of several weeks while on vacation. However, in the end, I have always had to go back because my work is essentially online. But I have a solution, in recent months I have been working on a project that allows me to separate my professional life from my personal life a little better. And, with it, leave Internet with everything that is not professional.

The main idea to achieve this is to understand that my life does not depend upon Internet. This means getting rid of all the content that comes from Internet platforms:

  • Goodbye social networks
  • Goodbye streaming and video services
  • Goodbye music and podcasts
  • Goodbye messaging apps
  • Goodbye personal email
  • Goodbye newspapers, blogs and newsletters
  • Goodbye algorithms for recommendations
  • Goodbye online stores and reviews

Goodbye to all information that comes from Internet.

I want to go back to the Internet that I grew up with, where I didn't desperately seek attention from others, where I didn't rely on others to make day-to-day decisions. On that Internet I had my own space where I offered content to others without waiting for feedback or other content in return.

A one-way channel

With this said, I have not completely disappeared from Internet, rather I have created a one-way channel. I am going to offer content to the community, but without any possibility of receiving feedback from it. For this, my website becomes a personal, safe and comfortable space to be present online. On my website you can see find for example the books that I read, photos about the beautiful city where I live, what I think, ██████ ████ or what I use.

Additionally, I am doing an interesting experiment in which I publish on Twitter without using Twitter. I have set up a system to post tweets via SMS. I don't even know if they are going to be published or not, even less what people think. They are personal tweets, from my real life and 100% free from the ephemeral trends of Twitter and Internet in general. The kind of tweets I was posting in 2007 when I wasn't craving for attention yet. And I love it.

The internet is wonderful, don't get me wrong. I appreciate with all my heart what humanity has done thanks to it. I'm just oversaturated and I want to enjoy the experiences and people that currently surround my life. And, as far, it's a great experiment.

From –the always beautiful– Auckland, NZ, I wish you all the best, Internet stranger.
